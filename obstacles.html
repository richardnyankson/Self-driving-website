Self-Driving Cars



Self-driving cars have emerged as a groundbreaking technology with the potential to revolutionize transportation. With advanced sensors, artificial intelligence, and sophisticated algorithms, these autonomous vehicles promise increased safety, improved efficiency, and enhanced mobility. However, as with any disruptive innovation, self-driving cars also face significant challenges that must be overcome for their widespread adoption. In the following paragraph, we will delve into some of the key obstacles and complexities that self-driving cars encounter in today's dynamic landscape.

Ethical Dilemmas
Ethical Dilemmas
Self-driving cars may face ethical dilemmas when confronted with difficult decisions on the road. For example, in a potential accident scenario, the car's algorithms may have to make split-second decisions that involve weighing the potential harm to different parties. Resolving these ethical challenges in a way that aligns with societal values and expectations is a significant hurdle.
