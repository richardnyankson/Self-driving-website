Timeline of the development of Self-Driving Cars

Timeline

Self-Driving cars have come a long way. This page shows the developments made in the past dating back to 1953 as well as the projections for the future. Move the slider to show the developments made in different years.

  1. 1953 GM and RCA develop a model automated highway to experiment with electronics to control steerage and distance between vehicles.
  2. 1958 GM tests a rudimentary self-steering Chevrolet.
  3. 1960s Stanford University roboticists develop the autonomous Stanford Cart based on the Lunar Rover platform.
  4. 1977 Dr Sadayuki Tsugawa unveils first fully AV equipped with cameras and analogue signal processing computers at the Mechanical Engineering Lab at Japan’s Ministry of International Trade and Industry.
  5. 1979 Stanford’s Hans Moravec’s Stanford Cart crosses a chair-filled room without incident or human intervention.
  6. 1987 Aerospace engineer Ernst Dickmanns’ VaMoRs vehicle outfitted with two cameras, 18 16-bit microprocessors, and a host of sensors self-drove for 20km at an average speed of 56mph.
  7. 1994 Dickmanns piloted a retrofitted Mercedes S-Class from Munich to Odense, covering roughly 95% of the distance autonomously.
  8. 1995 A team of CMU roboticists drove a 1990 Pontiac from Pittsburgh to LA with a 70-mile stretch completed without human help.
  9. 2005 Stanford University wins DARPA's challenge for robotic vehicles with its Stanley VW, completing the course in 6 hours and 54 minutes.
  10. 2009 Google starts testing robot cars on roads.
  11. 2015 Tesla unveils Autopilot. Baidu drives a retrofitted BMW 3 Series autonomously in Beijing. Delphi AV drives from coast to coast.
  12. 2016 Uber tests autonomous taxis with human co-pilot in Pittsburgh.
  13. 2017 NuTonomy self-drive taxis tested in Singapore. Gallup poll in the US shows majority public concern about AVs.
  14. 2018 March: Uber AV with human safety driver on board kills a pedestrian in Arizona. Autonomous lorries start to test on highways in the US and UK.
  15. 2018 Nissan-DNA launches self-driving experiment in Yokohama. Waymo runs a limited commercial robotaxi service in Chandler, Arizona.
  16. 2019 Tesla touted full autonomy would be available in its vehicles, it still has not achieved this target.
  17. 2020 GM launches Origin robotaxi with Lyft. Volvo and Uber announce plan for robo taxi service with Geely and Nvidia.
  18. 2025 Daimler aims to begin production of level 4 AVs with Bosch, Uber and Nvidia. Level 4 AVs are permitted to operate autonomously in prescribed areas in good conditions.
  19. 2025 Level 4 robotaxi ride hailing begins to be offered across the world’s urban areas, led by Didi Chuxing, Uber, Lyft, legacy original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and tech giants Apple, Amazon and Baidu.
  20. 2040 50% chance that the Omega point of electric vehicles (EVs), AVs and shared vehicles will be reached. More than 50% chance that level 5 vehicles will be available in the mass market.
